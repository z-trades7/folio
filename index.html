About Me

I've been drawn to mathematics since high school, where I first saw how numbers could solve real problems. As a baseball player, I learned what it means to work as part of a team—showing up every day, supporting teammates, and pushing through challenges together. Those experiences taught me that success rarely comes from individual effort alone.

I'm currently pursuing my bachelor's degree in finance, where I've found that the most meaningful learning happens when I dig into topics on my own. This led me to coding and web development, which turned into something I genuinely enjoy. I focus on building complex projects that solve real-world problems, combining technical skills with practical applications.

My coursework has given me a solid foundation in financial principles—from analyzing financial statements and conducting sensitivity analysis to evaluating capital budgeting decisions. I work with Python, SQL, and web technologies to apply these concepts in practical ways. Outside of coursework and coding, I spend time outdoors, help others when I can, and have a soft spot for animals. These keep me balanced and often provide perspective when I'm stuck on a problem.
